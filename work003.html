003 : Vision Based Robotic Arm Manipulation

Using offline Deep Reinforcement Learning to train Robotic Arm in performing sequential tasks.

Details:

Duration: December 2023 – Current

Program: Design Credit Project - Sem 4, Indian Institute of Technology Jodhpur

Instructors:

Professor Suril Shah, Sunil Kumar Yadav

Overview:

Building upon Sudir Yadav's research paper, our research endeavors will encompass a spectrum of experiments aimed at advancing the existing findings. Some of the key tasks on our agenda include:

Low-Level Control of Robots with Deep Learning: Exploring optimal strategies for low-level control of robots using Deep Learning techniques.

Enhancing Single Task Training: Experimenting with diverse strategies to improve single-task training, such as leveraging multiple cameras, depth information, and alternative state representations.

Exploring Transformer Architectures for Vision Tasks: Investigating the applicability of transformer architectures for learning vision-based tasks in robotics.

Reinforcement Learning in GYM Environment: Applying various RL methods (e.g. DQN, SAC) to train agents in the GYM environment, evaluating their performance and efficiency.

Offline vs Online Learning: Conducting a comparative analysis of offline and online learning, aiming to quantify the differences in terms of data requirements, computational resources, and overall performance.

Reward Variations: Investigating the impact of different reward structures, including one-time rewards versus continuous rewards, and exploring reward shaping techniques.

State Variations: Experimenting with variations in state representations, such as comparing robot state with image-based representations. This includes exploring options like varying image sizes, incorporating image history, adding wrist camera images, and depth images.

Simulator Exploration: Testing the model in various simulators to assess its robustness and generalization across different virtual environments.

Hardware Implementation: Implementing the developed models on hardware to evaluate their real-world applicability and performance.
